Legendary Folk Singer, Pete Seeger, Dies at 94


Pete Seeger died at New York-Presbyterian Hospital on Monday, January 27. The 94 year old folk singer was an activist for international disarmament, civil rights, counterculture and environmental causes.

The New York Times was only one of several news outlets to report on the musician's death. Other outlets include:

  • Huffington Post
  • Rolling Stone
  • CNN
  • NBC
  • ABC
  • CBS

The Pete Seeger Appreciation Page calls Seeger "America's best-loved folksinger...an untiring environmentalist...[and] a national treasure."

A memorial service was held at the Libby Funeral Home in Beacon NY on Sunday, February 2. According to USA Today, the memorial attracted more than 2,500 people.

Mercury News reported that the Freight and Salvage Coffeehouse in Berkeley, CA hosted a tribute to Seeger on Monday, February 3.

News outlets have continued to write about the folk singer's legacy, talking about how his music and politics were inextricably linked (The Washington Post) and showcasing videos of him performing with other musical legends, like Johnny and June Carter Cash (The LA Times).
